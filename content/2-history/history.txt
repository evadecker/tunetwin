Title: Borrowing or Stealing? The Exchange of Musical Ideas  Throughout Western History

----

Text: 

Today, composing a song that sounds too similar to an earlier work can destroy an artist’s credibility. Modern culture values the “creative genius” who can invent uniquely original compositions (and perform them, too). Borrowing musical ideas, once viewed as a practical tool in the creative process, is now viewed as stealing, an idea so firmly ingrained that there are hundreds of hotly-debated lawsuits each year over “intellectual property.” The notion of “owning” an idea is detrimental to creative output in a society.
